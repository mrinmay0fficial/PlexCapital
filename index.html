📖 What is SIP?

SIP means investing a fixed amount every month — like a recurring deposit, but in the stock market. You don't need a lump sum. Even ₹500/month is enough to start.

UnitsWhen you invest, you buy "units" of a fund. More units = more wealth over time.
12%The historical average return of Nifty 50 index. Use this as a benchmark.
RCARupee Cost Averaging — you automatically buy more when markets fall, which lowers your average cost.
💡 Pro TipStarting at 25 with ₹1,000/month beats starting at 35 with ₹5,000/month. Time is the real secret.

📖 What is Lump Sum?

You invest a large one-time amount and leave it to grow. Best used when you receive a bonus, gift, tax refund, or inheritance.

Rule of 72Divide 72 by your return % to know when money doubles. At 12%, money doubles every 6 years.
STPIf markets are high, use Systematic Transfer Plan — invest lump sum into liquid fund first, then transfer monthly to equity.
💡 Pro TipNever invest 100% of a windfall at once. Split: 50% lump sum now, 50% via STP over 6 months to reduce timing risk.

📖 What is CAGR?

CAGR is the "smoothed" yearly growth rate of any investment. It tells you how fast your money grew each year on average — useful for comparing different investments.

Nifty 5020-year CAGR ≈ 14–15%
FDCAGR ≈ 6–7.5%
Gold10-year CAGR ≈ 8–11%
Real EstateCity-dependent, roughly 6–10%
💡 Pro TipAlways compare investments by CAGR — not by total returns. A 3x return in 2 years is far better than a 3x return in 10 years.

📖 What is Goal SIP?

This is the reverse of SIP — instead of asking "how much will I get?", it asks "how much must I invest to reach my goal?" Perfect for planning a house, child's education, or retirement.

InflationAdd 20–30% extra to your goal to account for rising prices over the years.
Step-UpIncrease your SIP by 10% every year. This dramatically cuts the required monthly amount.
💡 ExampleWant ₹1 Crore in 20 years? You need just ~₹1,010/month at 15% return. Start today, not tomorrow.

📖 What is Home Loan EMI?

EMI (Equated Monthly Instalment) is the fixed amount you pay your bank every month to repay your home loan. It includes both the original loan amount (principal) and the bank's charges (interest).

PrincipalThe actual amount you borrowed from the bank.
InterestThe bank's fee for lending. In early years, 70–80% of your EMI is just interest!
Safe limitDon't take a home loan more than 3× your annual income.
💡 Pro TipTry to prepay extra in the first 5 years. That's when you save the most interest since principal reduction is highest then.

📖 What is Car / Personal Loan EMI?

Works exactly like home loan EMI — but for shorter durations. Car loans are typically 1–7 years. Personal loans can be for any purpose but come with higher interest rates.

Car loanUsually 8–12% interest. Keep tenure under 48 months to avoid overpaying.
Personal loan10–24% interest — the most expensive loan type. Avoid if possible.
Down paymentPay 20–30% upfront to reduce EMI and total interest paid.
⚠️ RememberA car loses value every year (depreciating asset). Shorter loan tenure = less interest wasted on something that's already losing worth.

📖 What is Loan Prepayment?

Prepayment means paying extra money towards your loan before the EMI date. Even a small extra payment directly reduces your outstanding principal and saves a huge amount of interest over time.

PrincipalEvery extra rupee you pay reduces the principal, which reduces future interest.
Part-prepayYou don't need to close the loan — even ₹50,000 once a year makes a big difference.
💡 Best TimePrepay in the first 5–7 years of a home loan. That's when your EMI is mostly interest, so every prepayment saves the most.

📖 Income Tax — Explained Simply

The government takes a percentage of your income as tax. How much depends on which "regime" (set of rules) you choose and your deductions.

New RegimeLower tax rates but you can't claim most deductions. Simpler. Good if your deductions are low.
Old RegimeHigher rates but allows deductions (80C, HRA, etc.). Better if deductions exceed ₹3.75L.
Section 80CInvest up to ₹1.5L in PPF, ELSS, LIC etc. and deduct it from taxable income.
Cess4% extra charge on your calculated tax. Goes to education and health funds.
Std. DeductionAuto ₹75,000 (New) or ₹50,000 (Old) deducted for all salaried people — no proof needed.
💡 Rule of ThumbIf your 80C + 80D + HRA + home loan interest adds up to more than ₹3.75L — choose Old Regime. Otherwise go New.

📖 What is HRA Exemption?

HRA (House Rent Allowance) is part of your salary given to cover rent. The government allows a portion of it to be tax-free — called the "exemption". This calculator tells you exactly how much you can save.

MetroDelhi, Mumbai, Chennai, Kolkata — exemption is 50% of Basic salary.
Non-MetroAll other cities — exemption is 40% of Basic salary.
The RuleExempt = lowest of: (HRA received) or (50%/40% of Basic) or (Rent paid − 10% of Basic).
💡 Did you know?You can pay rent to your own parents and still claim HRA — but you need a proper rent agreement and they must show it as income in their tax return.

📖 What is Capital Gains Tax?

When you sell an investment (stocks, mutual funds, property) for more than you paid, the profit is called a "capital gain" and the government taxes it.

STCGShort-Term Capital Gain — sold within 1 year (equity). Taxed at 20%.
LTCGLong-Term Capital Gain — held over 1 year (equity). Only 12.5% tax, and first ₹1.25L is tax-free!
Debt MFTaxed as per your income slab regardless of holding period (post April 2023).
PropertyLong-term if held 2+ years. 20% with indexation (adjusting for inflation).
💡 Key InsightHolding equity/equity MF for just 1 year can reduce your tax from 20% to 12.5% — and first ₹1.25L profit is completely tax-free every year.

📖 What is an FD?

Fixed Deposit (FD) is the simplest saving option — you give money to a bank for a fixed time period and they give it back with interest. Zero risk of losing money (up to ₹5L insured by DICGC).

CompoundingQuarterly compounding gives slightly more than annual. More frequent = more interest.
TDSIf FD interest exceeds ₹40,000/year, the bank automatically deducts 10% tax (TDS).
Senior CitizenBanks give 0.25–0.5% extra rate to people above 60 years.
💡 Smart MoveFD interest is taxable as per your slab. If you're in 30% slab, consider debt mutual funds instead — they're more tax-efficient.

📖 What is PPF?

PPF (Public Provident Fund) is a government savings scheme. It's completely safe, gives guaranteed interest, and — most importantly — is 100% tax-free at every stage.

EEEExempt-Exempt-Exempt: investment is tax-free (80C), interest is tax-free, maturity is tax-free. Best tax deal available.
Lock-in15 years. Partial withdrawal allowed from year 7 onwards. Full withdrawal only at maturity.
Max limitYou can invest max ₹1.5L per year. This also saves tax under Section 80C.
💡 Pro TipDeposit before the 5th of every month to earn interest for that month. Depositing on the 6th means you lose one full month of interest.

📖 What is an RD?

Recurring Deposit (RD) is like an FD but in monthly instalments. You deposit a fixed amount every month and get it back with interest after the tenure ends. Great for building a habit of saving.

vs FDFD = one lump sum. RD = monthly small amounts. RD rate is slightly lower than FD.
vs SIPRD gives fixed guaranteed returns. SIP invests in markets — higher potential but involves risk.
TaxRD interest is fully taxable as per your income slab, just like FD.
💡 Better AlternativeIf you can tolerate low risk, a SIP in a short-duration debt mutual fund gives better post-tax returns than RD, with similar liquidity.

How to Choose a Mutual Fund

Not all mutual funds are the same. Here's a plain-language guide to the types and what to look for — no jargon.

📈
Equity Mutual Funds
High Risk
Invests your money in company stocks. Highest potential returns over long term (10+ years). Best for wealth creation goals like retirement or child's education. Avoid if you need money in 1–3 years.
🏛️
Debt Mutual Funds
Low Risk
Invests in government bonds and company loans. Stable, predictable returns (6–8%). Good for 1–3 year goals or parking money you need soon. Interest is taxed at your income slab rate.
⚖️
Hybrid Funds
Moderate Risk
Mix of stocks + bonds. Balances growth with stability. Good for 3–5 year goals or first-time investors who want market exposure without full equity risk.
🗂️
Index Funds
Moderate Risk
Copies an index like Nifty 50 automatically. Very low fees (0.1–0.2%). Historically beats 80% of actively managed funds over 10 years. Best starting point for new investors.
💧
Liquid Funds
Very Low Risk
Better than savings account (6.5–7.5% returns). Money available within 1 business day. Perfect for your emergency fund or to park money for a short time.
💰
ELSS Funds
High Risk
Equity fund with a 3-year lock-in. Qualifies for Section 80C deduction (save up to ₹46,800 in tax). Shortest lock-in among all 80C options. High historical returns (12–18% CAGR).
📋 Before Investing: What to Check
1
Expense RatioThe annual fee you pay for the fund to be managed. Lower is better. Direct Plans have 0.1–0.5%; Regular Plans charge 1–2%. Always choose Direct Plan.
2
Direct vs Regular PlanRegular Plans are sold through agents who earn a commission from your returns. Direct Plans cut the middleman. Same fund, 0.5–1% more returns per year — huge over 20 years.
3
Investment Horizon (Your Time)Equity funds need 5+ years to smooth out market ups and downs. If you need money in 2 years, don't put it in equity — use debt or liquid funds.
4
Risk LevelEvery fund has a risk label: Low / Moderate / High / Very High. Match it to your comfort. If a 20% drop in one month would make you panic-sell, stay with moderate risk funds.
5
Exit LoadSome funds charge a fee (0.5–1%) if you withdraw money before a certain period (usually 1 year). Always check this before investing.

⚠️ This is general financial education. Past performance doesn't guarantee future returns. Consult a SEBI-registered advisor before investing.

Investment Tips

Practical wisdom to grow your wealth steadily, even on a regular salary.

Investing
Start SIP with even ₹500/month
You don't need lakhs to start investing. A ₹500 SIP in an index fund grows to ₹15L+ in 30 years at 12% returns. The habit matters more than the amount.
Investing
Index funds beat most active funds long-term
Over a 10-year period, 80%+ of actively managed funds underperform the Nifty 50 index. An index fund with 0.1% expense ratio beats a fund with 2% ratio by enormous margins.
Investing
Asset allocation: the only free lunch
Divide your portfolio: 60% equity for growth, 30% debt for stability, 10% gold as hedge. Rebalance every year. This single rule can protect you in market crashes.
Investing
Never time the market, time in the market
Staying invested through market crashes always outperforms trying to sell and rebuy at the bottom. Missing the top 10 best days in NIFTY halves your 20-year returns.
Savings
Build a 6-month emergency fund first
Before investing, park 6 months of expenses in a liquid fund or high-interest savings account. This prevents you from redeeming long-term investments in a crisis.
Savings
The 50-30-20 budget rule
50% of take-home on needs (rent, food), 30% on wants (dining, travel), 20% on savings/investments. This one rule changes your financial life if followed consistently.
Investing
Step-up your SIP by 10% every year
If you step up your SIP by 10% annually, your corpus can be 2–3x larger than a flat SIP. As your salary grows, your investments should grow with it.
Investing
Invest in NPS for guaranteed retirement
NPS gives additional ₹50,000 deduction under 80CCD(1B) beyond 80C limits. At low cost and with equity exposure, it's ideal for a secure retirement corpus.
